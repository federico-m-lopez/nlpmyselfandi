In practice stress test exercises aim at assessing the capacity of financial institutions to absorb financial and economic shocks. In regular exercises such as the ones conducted by the European Banking Authority ORG the shocks are usually considered under so called central and baseline macroeconomic scenarios corresponding to a median quantile and adverse scenario usually taken as a 90 th percentile reflecting severe yet plausible scenario that can occur once every 10 years 30 . Additionally extreme scenarios can be considered for measuring the capital adequacy 31 for absorbing extremely severe losses around confidence level at 99.9 CARDINAL . From a clearing member perspective this requires to have the capacity of scanning certain points of its trading loss distribution. In our framework this boils down to identifying particular levels of the distribution of the trading loss C CVA PRODUCT C CCVA BCVA of the reference clearing member bank where the different terms are detailed in Proposition 3.2 LAW .
